Japan "Yes" to Death Penalty?

"Hanging" is a method of the execution

By Mayumi Oshige | July 23, 2023 | Github

The death penalty has been carried out almost every year in Japan since 1882(the Meiji era) according to Crime Info based on the Annual Report on Statistics of Prosecutions in Japan. Although it has been on the decline in recent years, a double-digit number of people were executed in 2018 for the first time in quite a while.

In Japan, the death penalty is required by criminal code. Punishments and the method of execution is by "hanging". An analysis of "death penalty by country" in Wikipedia shows that 38 countries out of 197 UN member states have adopted hanging. Most are concentrated in Asian and African countries.

Amnesty International has released a report of 88 countries that put people to death in 2022. Japan is in this report with one recorded death. China is estimated to have executed over 1,000 and Iran is over 500 people. The UN has beein calling on member states to halt executions.

The 2019 poll of Japan showed that 80.8% of responders said that the death penalty was "unavoidable" -- meaning "Yes" to death penalty. Only 9% said it should be abolished and 10% were unsure. However, the bar association noted that of those who said "Yes" to death penalty, nealry 40% answered "it may be abolished in the future". They take issue with the wording of the question and call for more public information and discussion about death penalty.
