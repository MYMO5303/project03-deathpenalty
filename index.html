Japan "Yes" to Death Penalty?

"Hanging" is a method of the execution

By Mayumi Oshige | July 23, 2023 | Github

The death penalty has been carried out almost every year in Japan since 1882(the Meiji era). According to Crime Info based on the Annual Report on Statistics of Prosecutions in Japan. Although it has been on the decline in recent years, a double-digit number of people were executed in 2018.

In Japan, the death penalty is required by law. Punishments and the method of execution is by "hanging". An analysis of "death penalty by country" in Wikipedia shows that out of 196 UN member states, at least 38 countries including Japan have adopted hanging. Most are concentrated in Asian and African countries.

Amnesty International has released a report of 88 countries that put people to death in 2022. Japan is in this report with one recorded death. China is estimated to have executed over 1,000 and Iran is over 500 people. The UN has called on member states to halt executions.

However, a public opinion poll conducted by Japan in 2019 showed that 80.8% of responders said that the death penalty was "unavoidable" -- meaning "Yes" to death penalty. Only 9% percent said it should be abolished, and 10 percent were unsure. Japan is one of the countries that is in opposition of the UN death penalty moratirum.
